Urban Seeds - UX Research

Urban Seeds is a conceptual UI/UX project, designed to educate and support all levels of gardeners throughout every stage of their journey. In this project, I was responsible for the user research and surveys, as well as the promotional website and video.

User Survey

Our survey consisted of a series of questions designed to gauge our usersâ€™ gardening experience level, background, interest in gardening, demographic, personal info, and more. We sent out a broad survey to the general public which we called Group 1. In addition, we shared the survey in a few Texas Gardening Facebook groups, which we called Group 2.

  • What environment do you live in?
  • Have you ever wanted to start a garden?
  • Have you ever grown your own food?
  • Does the idea of starting a garden intimidate you?
  • What types of gardens have you done?
  • What are some of the biggest challenges for you?
  • What are features you would like in a gardening app?
  • How would you rate the success of your garden?

Pain Points

Included below are the common pain points we identified in our surveys, across both Group 1 and Group 2.

  • Time (getting started)
  • Lack of success after trying
  • Not knowing where to start & intimidation
  • Lack of gardening knowledge

User Personas

Our survey responses formed the basis for the following personas.

Caitlyn Gomez

  • 22 year old college student
  • Living in apartment
  • Has never gardened - doesn't know where to start
  • Wants to start with houseplants
  • Needs guidance on where to start and what to buy
  • Keep garden alive with minimal effort

Susanna Harrison

  • 57 year old woman in a rural house
  • Lots of yard space
  • Moved to Texas from Oregon
  • Struggling with Texas weather
  • Has difficulty with mobility
  • Wants to simplify and organize her large garden
  • Wants to learn how to fight off Texas pests

Jacob Whitler

  • 42 year old father of 2 living in the suburbs
  • Busy - can only tend garden on weekends
  • Doesn't like pesticides from grocery store produce
  • Minimal yard space
  • Needs to optimize time and space

Reflections

Overall, this project exposed me to the value, and also challenges, of user research. Features that we might have previously implemented, or not implemented, in our app were given a different perspective based on our surveys. In addition, I gained multiple tips from the Texas Gardeners that I would not have otherwise thought of, and that proved to be crucial.
